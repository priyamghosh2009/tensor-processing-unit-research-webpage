About the Researcher

Priyam Ghosh is an independent software developer and AI hardware enthusiast focused on computer architecture and next-generation computing systems.

He completed his Class XII education in 2026 from Delhi Public School, Paradip Refinery, and is currently 17 years old.

His early development journey began with C and C++ graphics programming, later expanding into full-stack web systems, educational platforms, and experimental operating systems. These projects formed the foundation for his current research direction in AI hardware and Instruction Set Architecture (ISA) design.

He is currently pursuing independent research on Tensor Processing Unit (TPU) architecture, with a target to complete a fully defined ISA by December 2026.

Abstract

This project proposes a Tensor Processing Unit (TPU) Instruction Set Architecture optimized for AI workloads. The goal is to reduce inefficiencies in modern AI computation by designing a tensor-native instruction model that bridges software frameworks and hardware execution systems.

Research Vision

We aim to create an architecture where AI computation becomes:

• Faster
• More energy efficient
• Hardware-native
• Universally accessible
